Sancocho

Description

Sancocho is a traditional stew or soup popular in several Latin American countries, such as Colombia, the Dominican Republic, Panama, and Venezuela. It’s a hearty dish made with a mix of meats (like chicken, beef, pork, or fish) and a variety of starchy vegetables, such as yuca, plantains, potatoes, yams, and corn on the cob. It’s seasoned with herbs like cilantro, onions, and garlic, and often served with avocado or a squeeze of lime on the side. The recipe varies by region, but it’s always a comforting, flavorful, and nutritious meal. It’s a true culinary delight!

Ingredients

  • 500 g of chicken, pork, or beef (or a mix)
  • 2 green plantains, peeled and chopped
  • 2 yucas, peeled and chopped
  • 1 yam, peeled and chopped
  • 1 sweet potato, peeled and chopped
  • 2 corn cobs, cut into pieces
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon of oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Steps

  1. In a large pot, boil the chicken, pork, or beef with enough water until fully cooked.

  2. Add the plantains, yuca, yam, sweet potato, and corn. Cook for 30 minutes or until the vegetables are tender.

  3. Add the onion, garlic, oregano, salt, and pepper. Cook for another 15 minutes.

  4. Serve hot, with a side of white rice and avocado.
