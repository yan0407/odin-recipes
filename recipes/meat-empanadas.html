Meat Empanadas


Description

Meat empanadas are a popular and delicious dish from the Dominican Republic. They are made with a crispy, golden dough, typically prepared with wheat flour, and filled with a savory meat mixture. While beef is the most common filling, chicken or pork can also be used. The meat is seasoned with ingredients like onions, garlic, bell peppers, tomatoes, cilantro, oregano, and spices, creating a flavorful and aromatic filling. Some variations may include olives, raisins, or hard-boiled eggs for added texture and taste.

The empanadas are deep-fried until perfectly crispy and served hot. They are a staple at parties, family gatherings, and as street food, loved for their satisfying crunch and rich flavor. Whether filled with beef, chicken, or pork, these empanadas are a true Dominican favorite!

Ingredients

  • 2 cups of all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup of vegetable oil (for dough)
  • 1/4 cup of water
  • 1/2 lb of ground beef
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • 1 teaspoon of cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon of paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Vegetable oil for frying

Steps

  1. In a pan, saut√© the ground beef with onions and garlic until the meat is browned and the onions are soft.

  2. Season with cumin, paprika, salt, and pepper. Cook for another 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally.

  3. In a separate bowl, mix the flour, vegetable oil, and water to form the dough. Knead until smooth.

  4. Divide the dough into small balls and roll each one into a thin circle.

  5. Place a spoonful of the ground beef mixture in the center of each circle, then fold over to form a half-moon shape.

  6. Seal the edges with a fork and fry in hot oil until golden brown and crispy, about 3-4 minutes per side.
