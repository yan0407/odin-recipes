Tostones

Description

Tostones are a popular Caribbean dish made from green plantains that are sliced, fried, flattened, and then fried again until golden and crispy. They are typically served as a side dish or appetizer, often accompanied by garlic sauce, mayo-ketchup, or other dips. Tostones have a crunchy texture and a slightly salty, starchy taste, making them a staple in Puerto Rican, Dominican, and Cuban cuisine.

Ingredients

  • 1 green plantain
  • 5 tablespoons oil for frying
  • 3 cups cold water
  • salt to taste

Steps

  1. Gather all ingredients.

  2. Peel plantain and cut into 1-inch slices. Fill a bowl with 3 cups of cold water.

  3. Heat oil in a large deep skillet over medium-high heat; add plantain slices in an even layer and fry on both sides until golden brown, about 3 1/2 minutes per side. Set skillet aside.

  4. Transfer plantain slices to a chopping board; flatten each one by placing a small plate on top and pressing down.

  5. Dip plantain slices in cold water.

  6. Reheat oil in the skillet over medium heat; cook plantain slices for 1 minute on each side.

  7. Season to taste with salt and serve immediately.
